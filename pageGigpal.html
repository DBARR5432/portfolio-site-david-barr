GigPal

GigPal is a native app for Android and iOS that will provide a social network specifically for musicians, bands, and venues to aid in the process of finding one another, and getting gigs booked.

My Process

Research

For GigPal I focused on bulletin board apps that allow posts, mainly Reddit but also Facebook and Instagram. I also took a look at Facebook/Instagram’s messaging feature, and calendars from iOS and Android.

Ideation

After the research phase, I decided on three MVP features. These were ability to create a profile, post personal ads, and communicate with other users. Search and Calendar were included as secondary features. Paper wireframes created at this point for each user flow, in both iOS and Android versions.

Wireframes

Once I had the paper wireframes where I wanted them, I created digital greyscale versions in Figma. This was my first time using components for Material and Human Interface Guidelines out of kits. Once I got comfortable with using them it was clear how they would ultimately speed up my work flow.

Design

From the greyscale I applied a full color palette, imagery, and typography to each version. More screens were added to help fill out the prototype. Once that was completed I sought out user feedback on the prototype, having subjects work through the MVP features.

Prototype Feedback

The user testing proved invaluable, and the most critical and necessary changes were implemented with the final design.

Conclusion

Challenges

  • Personally my experience with iOS is very limited compared to Android, so there was more difficulty and research involved with utilizing common iOS UI patterns.
  • Calendar feature was more involved than initially thought. Had to create more screens than anticipated and create custom components for those screens.
  • Creating a functional hamburger menu for the Android prototype required some experimentation.
  • Having to “reverse engineer” certain components take from UI kits showed I have much more to learn about component states and capabilities in Figma.

Takeaways

  • Getting outside user feedback as early and frequently as possible should be a goal on every project.
  • I still have a great deal to learn in anticipating problems later on in a project, but keeping things simple, both with styling and components, can allow for easier changes to be made later. Undoing something complex will be far more involved and time consuming.
  • Continue to study and prioritize Material and Human Interface Guidelines. I think I could’ve done worse following the guidelines, but I also know I could’ve done better.
  • I’m improving in certain aspects of project organization, namely utilizing styles for color, effects, etc. There are others that still need improvement like my tracking of icons and components in my design file.
